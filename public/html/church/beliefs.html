What We Believe

Our Beliefs

  • God

    One God in three persons—Father, Son, and Holy Spirit—eternal, almighty, and merciful.

  • The Bible

    God’s inspired and inerrant Word, our final authority for faith and life.

  • Creation

    God created all things by His Word and sustains His creation with care.

  • Man & Woman

    Humanity is created in God’s image with dignity, designed for relationship with Him.

  • Sin

    All have sinned and fall short of God’s glory, separated from Him without grace.

  • The Gospel

    The good news that Jesus lived, died, and rose to save sinners—God’s gift to us.

  • Jesus

    True God and true man, the promised Savior who redeems us by His cross and empty tomb.

  • Salvation

    By grace alone through faith alone in Christ alone—not by works—so no one may boast.

  • Faith

    The Holy Spirit creates and strengthens faith through the Word and Sacraments.

  • The Church

    All believers in Christ, gathered by His Word, sent to proclaim His grace.

  • Baptism & Holy Communion

    Gospel gifts that deliver forgiveness and strengthen faith by God’s promise.

  • Peace

    In Christ we have peace with God now and the hope of eternal life.

The Word of God

We believe the Holy Scriptures are the inspired and inerrant Word of God. They are His perfect revelation and the only foundation for faith and life.

  • God speaks through Scripture—His law convicts, His gospel comforts.
  • The Bible alone tells us who God is, what He has done, and how we are saved.
  • All teaching in the Church must agree with this Word of truth.

The Triune God

There is one eternal God—Father, Son, and Holy Spirit—three persons, yet one divine being, equal in power, glory, and majesty.

  • The Father is our Creator and sustainer of all things.
  • The Son, Jesus Christ, is true God and true man, our Redeemer.
  • The Holy Spirit creates and strengthens saving faith through the Word and Sacraments.

The Problem

God made everything perfect. But through human disobedience came sin and death. Because of sin, our relationship with God was broken, and we are powerless to repair it on our own.

The Solution

Out of love, God sent His Son Jesus to live the perfect life we never could, to suffer and die in our place, and to rise again in victory over sin and death. His resurrection is the guarantee of our forgiveness and eternal life.

The Good News

God loves you deeply. Through Jesus, He offers full forgiveness and eternal life as a free gift. Nothing is required—nothing but faith that trusts in Christ’s finished work. This is the gospel. This is good news. Believe it!

Faith Alone

We are justified by grace through faith alone—not by works. Faith is not our doing but the gift of God, created by the Holy Spirit through the Word.

  • Faith receives the righteousness of Christ as our own.
  • Good works flow naturally from faith as fruits of gratitude.

The Church

The Church is all believers in Christ throughout the world, gathered around the Word and Sacraments. It exists to proclaim the gospel and to serve in love.

  • We gather to worship, learn, and encourage one another.
  • Through Baptism and the Lord’s Supper, God assures us of His grace.
  • The Church’s mission is to make disciples of all nations.
